Developing a Football Player Injury Prediction Model

Universitat Politècnica de Catalunya (UPC)

Anticipating player injuries has become a crucial priority for professional clubs. This project explores the application of machine learning algorithms, such as Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) and Support Vector Machines (SVM), to predict injuries based solely on official match statistics.

Using web scraping techniques to collect data from specialized platforms, and after a thorough process of data cleaning, dimensionality reduction, and model training, a system was developed to detect patterns that could signal the risk of muscular injuries. Although the model faces challenges—especially due to the lack of training data and the natural imbalance between injured and non-injured players—the results highlight the promising potential of artificial intelligence as a proactive tool for injury prevention in professional sports.
